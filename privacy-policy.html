Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Elama Consulting ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your personal information when you visit www.elamaconsulting.com or engage with our services.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of information:

  • Contact information — your name, email address, company name, and any message you submit through our contact form.
  • Usage data — pages visited, time spent on the site, and browser/device information collected through standard web analytics.
  • Communication records — any correspondence you send us directly by email or through our booking system.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely to:

  • Respond to your enquiries and provide the services you request.
  • Schedule and conduct consultations or coaching sessions.
  • Send you relevant updates or resources you have opted in to receive.
  • Improve the quality and performance of our website.

3. We Will Never Sell Your Data

We will never sell, rent, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties for commercial purposes. Your data belongs to you. We treat it with the same confidentiality we expect in our own professional relationships.

4. Data Sharing

We do not share your personal information with outside parties except in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers — trusted tools we use to operate our business (such as our scheduling platform and email service), who are contractually bound to keep your information confidential and may not use it for any other purpose.
  • Legal obligation — if required to do so by law or in response to a valid legal request from a government authority.

5. Data Storage and Security

Your information is stored securely using industry-standard encryption and access controls. We retain your data only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. We do not store payment details — all transactions are handled by our third-party payment processors.

6. Cookies

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience and analyse site traffic. You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. Note that some features of the site may not function properly if cookies are disabled.

7. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (such as our booking platform or partner sites). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their privacy policies before submitting any personal information.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • Request correction of any inaccurate information.
  • Request deletion of your personal data, subject to any legal obligations we may have to retain it.
  • Opt out of any marketing communications at any time by replying to any email with "unsubscribe" or by contacting us directly.

9. Children's Privacy

Our services are intended for business professionals and are not directed at individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this page periodically to stay informed about how we are protecting your information.
